# Introductory Statistics: with LLMs
## Compact Tik TOC Architecture

**Book type:** Course textbook  
**Status:** Retrospective architecture for a developed manuscript  

## Core Thesis

This book teaches introductory statistics as disciplined reasoning under uncertainty, with LLMs used to explain, practice, simulate, and critique statistical thinking without replacing the learner's responsibility to understand assumptions and interpretation.

## Primary Reader

Undergraduate students, graduate students needing a refresher, and practitioners who need to interpret data, tests, intervals, models, and uncertainty.

## Learning Arc

1. **Describe and model variation:** data, sampling, descriptive statistics, probability, and distributions.
2. **Infer under uncertainty:** sampling distributions, confidence intervals, hypothesis tests, and errors.
3. **Compare and model relationships:** two-sample tests, chi-square, ANOVA, regression, and correlation.

## Chapter Architecture

Each chapter should move from a concrete question to a statistical tool, then to interpretation and misuse. LLM prompts should ask for explanation, simulation, assumption checks, and plain-English interpretation.

## Terminal Capability

The reader can choose common statistical procedures, interpret their outputs, explain uncertainty, and avoid overclaiming what the data supports.

## Adoption Risks

- LLM explanations can hide mathematical gaps.
- Students may confuse calculation with interpretation.
- The course must protect practice without AI assistance.

## Production Notes

Use Tik TOC to strengthen through-lines: uncertainty, assumptions, visual inspection, and claim calibration.
